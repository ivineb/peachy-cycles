Problem Statement

The bicycling industry in the US is known to be dominated by white men. Women, people of color, and the LGBTQIA+ community find biking an intimidating activity with an uninviting environment.

The main reasons why they refrain from cycling are: feeling unsafe on the roads, lacking knowledge, skills and resources, and finally not being able to join a cycling group or be a part of a community. Most groups are competitive, dominated by white men, and intimidating.


Project Goal

Peachy Cycles aims to empower, educate and provide connection, support and resources to the underserved communities. This platform equips cyclists with tools and knowledge through different resources such as tips & tricks articles, routes sharing, gear reviews, bike fitting sessions, and maintenance workshops. Connecting cyclists together and to bike shops will help create events and build diverse cycling teams. The goal is to create a tight-knit cycling community where everyone feels welcomed and empowered.


Project Medium

The deliverable for this project is a website containing different sections that empowers and educates riders, and focuses on the community. Getting people involved helps building a more sustainable & inclusive bicycling platform!
